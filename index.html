About Me

I'm interested in "big data" systems, parallel programming models, and privacy/security algorithms. My approach to work is primarily systems and software-oriented: I identify real-world problems, design and implement a novel solution in software, and experimentally validate the software. I also happen to work at Oak Ridge National Lab as a research scientist.

Projects

I have both "official" and "for fun" projects.

  • I (and many of my colleagues at ORNL) have been helping the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) modernize their analytic capabilities. Here are some links from the press: http://gcn.com/articles/2012/03/09/cms-analytics-project-health-care-big-data.aspx http://technews.tmcnet.com/knowledge-management/topics/knowledge-management/articles/303131-cms-unveils-knowledge-discovery-infrastructure-aims-patient-data.htm
  • Spot Hadoop: Spot-Hadoop is a tool to dynamically configure, launch, and benchmark Hadoop on shared, parallel filesystems (namely Lustre).
  • Paja: Paja is an implementation of Paxos for Java.
  • KevaDB: KevaDB is a key-value storage library for Java with support for multiple branches and historical values.
